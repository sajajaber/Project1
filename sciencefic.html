  1. Project Hail Mary by Andy Weir: An amnesiac astronaut wakes up on a spaceship hurtling towards a distant star system, with no memory of his mission or how he got there. Packed with Weir's signature blend of scientific accuracy and nail-biting suspense, this is a journey you won't want to miss.

  2. Leviathan Wakes by James S.A. Corey: In a future where humanity has colonized the solar system, tensions rise between Earth and Mars. When a mysterious incident throws the fragile peace into jeopardy, two unlikely heroes – a jaded detective and a hotshot pilot – must team up to unravel the truth before it's too late. This is the gripping first book in the Expanse series, a New York Times bestselling space opera.
  1. The Martian by Andy Weir: Astronaut Mark Watney gets stranded on Mars after a fierce storm separates him from his crew. With limited supplies and ingenuity as his only weapons, Watney must overcome seemingly insurmountable odds to survive and find a way to signal for help. This thought-provoking and inspiring story will leave you in awe of the human spirit.

  2. A Fire Upon the Deep by Vernor Vinge: Spanning millennia and galaxies, this epic saga explores the profound questions of consciousness, existence, and the nature of reality. Vinge's masterful storytelling and mind-bending concepts will make you ponder the universe's deepest mysteries long after you turn the last page.
  1. The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams: This wacky and irreverent space comedy follows Arthur Dent, a hapless Englishman who becomes the sole survivor of Earth's destruction. With his two-headed alien friend Ford Prefect, Arthur embarks on a hilarious journey through the galaxy, encountering bizarre creatures, absurd situations, and the ultimate answer to the ultimate question (which is, of course, 42).

  2. Redshirts by John Scalzi: Have you ever wondered what it's like to be a redshirt on Star Trek – those nameless crew members who always seem to die horrible deaths? Scalzi takes us behind the scenes of a typical starship adventure, where the redshirts are just trying to do their jobs (and avoid getting killed) while dealing with incompetent captains, malfunctioning technology, and alien attacks. This witty and satirical romp will have you laughing out loud.
